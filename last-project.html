As parents,we all want the very best for our kids. We get them involved in various activities that will enrich their childhood while developing life skills that transcend into adulthood.

By playing Chess, kids and adults can improve and develop cognitive skills.

Excercise The Brain

Playing chess requires utilizing the entire brain towards developing both the left and right hemispheres,which is crucial for young minds.

Improve Spatial Skills

being able to calculate moves ten steps ahead and determine the possible outcomes of each move is a form of spatial skill development

Improves Memory

Chess is a game of strategy,with pieces that move in specific patterns. These patterns must be memorized in order to play. training the brain to remember and retain information gives it stregnth.

The king is basically the game,it's a game over when he has no move to make. He moves a step horizontally,vertically and diagonally.

the queen is the most strongest worrior in the game. She moves all the way horizontally, vertically,and diagonally.

The rook is also a strong piece, he moves both horizontally and vertically,he is also involved in castling.

The bishops are placed on a black diagonal line and a white diagonal line.
